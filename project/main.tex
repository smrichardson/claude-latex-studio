\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
One paragraph synthesizing what these papers collectively say and the gap you
see across them. Write this last, once the sections below are filled in.
\end{abstract}

\section{Motivation}
Why this body of work matters and the question that ties these papers together.

\section{Themes}
Group the papers by idea, not just one-after-another. Each subsection is a
thread that several papers speak to.

\subsection{Thread one}
Paper A \citep{keyA} proposes \dots{} Its key result is \dots{}

\subsection{Thread two}
Paper B \citep{keyB} instead argues \dots{} Compared with \citet{keyA}, it \dots{}

\section{Comparison}
Where the papers agree, where they disagree, and the tradeoffs between their
approaches (assumptions, data, cost, generality).

\section{Gaps \& directions}
Open problems the literature has not resolved, and what you would do next.
